01. Booting Your Drive

01 Plug In
Insert into a USB 3.0 (Blue) port. If on a laptop, plug in your power cable for max speed.
02 The Trigger
Power on and repeatedly tap your Boot Menu Key:
DELL / LENOVOF12
HP / COMPAQF9 or ESC
ASUS / ACERF2 or F12

The #1 Fix: Disable Secure Boot

If your PC skips the USB and goes straight to Windows, you must disable "Secure Boot" in your BIOS. This is a security lock that often blocks non-Windows software.

To fix: Enter BIOS (usually tap F2 or Del at startup), find "Secure Boot", set it to Disabled, and Save/Exit.

02. Essential Commands

Open the "Terminal" app in your Linux menu and try these basics:

lsList all files in your current folder.
sudo apt install [app]Install new software (vlc, discord, etc).
lsblkSee all connected drives and USB sticks.
df -hCheck how much free space is left on your NIXKEY.
